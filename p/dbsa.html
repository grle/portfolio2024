DBSA SILICON VALLEY

This is a website for the Silicon Valley chapter of the Depression Bipolar Support Alliance. This organization’s main purpose is to provide support groups for people struggling with mood disorders, while this chapter also has social events to encourage community building. The goal of the website is to give information to users regarding the services that this organization provides.

Role

UX Designer,

Frontend Developer

Team

Worked with the leadership team at DBSA Silicon Valley, including Chantell Petrell (president) and Kurtis Reiner (treasurer)

Timeframe

2 months

How do you make a simple and aesthetic website that informs users on how to find the right support for their mental health?

User Research

Here’s what we know about the people in our organization.

Most were already familiar with DBSA and came in based on their trust regarding the organization’s name recognition. While our organization aims to host weekly support groups for people with mood disorders, anyone who is struggling with their mental illness is welcome.

Based on conversations with our participants, we were able to determine the following needs for our website:

  • Time and Zoom link for our support groups
  • Information about activities or community events
  • Mental health resources

Because we are a chapter of DBSA National, I used the websites of various DBSA state and local chapters to do a Comparative Analysis for our website.

Website Ideation

We started by doing an ideation session on everything we could do as an organization. We wanted to dream big, to have an ambitious vision, before we started thinking about the practicalities. Afterward, I translated this to our website sitemap that could meet our needs before moving on to the wireframes.

Writing Styleguide

We wanted to give people an idea of what to expect from the support group through the website, and part of how I did it was in the content writing. As support group facilitators, we are expected to speak and conduct ourselves in a certain way, so I created a writing styleguide to mimic that communication pattern in the copywriting. This adds to the general theme I wanted to convey on the website, which is warm and welcoming while still being professional.

Website Styleguide

I used warm-colored images while sticking to a default blue for the website colors. Since DBSA National’s styleguide is strong on blue and white, I wanted to align with our parent organization’s theme.

HTML, CSS, Javascript

I had the opportunity to practice my coding chops by creating a static website using HTML, CSS, and Javascript and pushing it on Github. Once I was finished, it was a simple matter of publishing our website.

DBSA Silicon Valley Website

Reflection

100% of feedback regarding the changes have been positive:

  • Users have reported that this updated design is easy to use and navigate. Through feedback surveys, the client has reported an increase in positive responses.
  • Stakeholders were pleased with the results, and have praised the updated product for its minimalist design and simplicity.
  • Instructors have noted that the updates have helped their sessions run more smoothly and expressed renewed excitement on working with a more user friendly product.

We are happy to see that our clients are happy. Of course, there is always room for more improvement. There are already talks on what changes can be made for the next iteration, specifically about reshooting some of the lectures and updating the language used in the learning material.
